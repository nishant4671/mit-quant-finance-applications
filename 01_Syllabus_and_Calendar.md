# MIT 18.642: Syllabus and Calendar

## Course Information
**Course:** Topics in Mathematics with Applications in Finance (Updated from Fall 2013 18.S096)
**Lectures:** 2 sessions/week, 1.5 hours/session

### Prerequisites
* 18.03 Differential Equations (or approval of instructor)
* 18.05 Introduction to Probability and Statistics OR 18.600 Probability and Random Variables
* 18.06 Linear Algebra
* *Helpful but not required:* Broad familiarity with linear algebra, statistics, stochastic processes, partial differential equations, economics, or finance.

### Course Description
The purpose of the class is to expose undergraduate and graduate students to the mathematical concepts and techniques used in the financial industry. The course consists of a set of mathematics lectures on topics in linear algebra, probability, statistics, stochastic processes, and numerical methods, mixed with lectures illustrating the corresponding application in the financial industry. 
* **MIT mathematicians** teach the mathematics part.
* **Industry professionals** (from investment banks, FinTech, hedge funds) teach the applications.

### Requirements & Grading
* **40%** - Five assignments covering math lecture topics.
* **20%** - Mid-semester group project (lecture note and in-class presentation).
* **30%** - Final paper on a math finance topic of the student's choice (replaces final exam).
* **10%** - Participation.

### Instructors
* **Dr. Peter Kempthorne:** Lecturer in MIT Dept. of Mathematics; President of Kempthorne Analytics; former Principal Research Scientist at MIT Sloan.
* **Dr. Vasily Strela:** Managing Director and Global Head of Fixed Income Quantitative Analytics at RBC Capital Markets; Research Affiliate at MIT Math.
* **Dr. Jake Xia:** Managing Director of Harvard Management Company; former Managing Director of Morgan Stanley; Research Affiliate at MIT Math.
