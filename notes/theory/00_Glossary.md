# 📖 Glossary — Key Terms in Quantitative Finance

> Add definitions here as you encounter new terms. Keep it alphabetical.

---

## A

| Term | Definition | First Seen (Week) |
|------|------------|--------------------|
| Arbitrage | A risk-free profit from price differences in two or more markets. | – |
| Alpha | Excess return of an investment relative to a benchmark index. | – |

## B

| Term | Definition | First Seen (Week) |
|------|------------|--------------------|
| Basis Point (bp) | 1/100th of a percent (0.01%). Used to express changes in interest rates. | – |
| Beta | A measure of a security's volatility relative to the overall market. | – |
| Black-Scholes | A model for pricing European-style options, based on stochastic calculus. | – |
| Brownian Motion | A continuous-time stochastic process; the foundation of many financial models. | – |

## C

| Term | Definition | First Seen (Week) |
|------|------------|--------------------|
| CAPM | Capital Asset Pricing Model — relates expected return to systematic risk (beta). | – |
| Convexity | A measure of the curvature in the relationship between bond prices and yields. | – |
| Correlation | A statistical measure of how two securities move in relation to each other (−1 to +1). | – |

## D–F

| Term | Definition | First Seen (Week) |
|------|------------|--------------------|
| Delta | Rate of change of option price with respect to the underlying asset's price. | – |
| Duration | Weighted-average time until a bond's cash flows are received; measures interest rate sensitivity. | – |
| EWMA | Exponentially Weighted Moving Average — gives more weight to recent observations. | – |

## G–I

| Term | Definition | First Seen (Week) |
|------|------------|--------------------|
| GARCH | Generalized Autoregressive Conditional Heteroskedasticity — a volatility model. | – |
| Greeks | Sensitivities of option price to various factors (Delta, Gamma, Theta, Vega, Rho). | – |
| Itô's Lemma | A key result in stochastic calculus for finding the differential of a function of a stochastic process. | – |

## J–L

| Term | Definition | First Seen (Week) |
|------|------------|--------------------|
| Log-normal | A distribution where the logarithm of the variable is normally distributed. | – |

## M–O

| Term | Definition | First Seen (Week) |
|------|------------|--------------------|
| Martingale | A stochastic process where the expected future value equals the current value. | – |
| Monte Carlo | A simulation method using repeated random sampling to estimate numerical results. | – |

## P–R

| Term | Definition | First Seen (Week) |
|------|------------|--------------------|
| Portfolio | A collection of financial assets (stocks, bonds, derivatives, etc.). | – |
| Put Option | A contract giving the right (not obligation) to sell an asset at a specified price. | – |

## S

| Term | Definition | First Seen (Week) |
|------|------------|--------------------|
| Sharpe Ratio | Risk-adjusted return: (Return − Risk-Free Rate) / Standard Deviation. | – |
| Stochastic | Involving randomness or probability; not deterministic. | – |
| Stochastic Differential Equation (SDE) | A differential equation with one or more stochastic (random) terms. | – |

## T–V

| Term | Definition | First Seen (Week) |
|------|------------|--------------------|
| Theta | Rate of change of option price with respect to time (time decay). | – |
| Value at Risk (VaR) | The maximum expected loss over a given time period at a given confidence level. | – |
| Volatility | A statistical measure of the dispersion of returns; often measured as standard deviation. | – |

## W–Z

| Term | Definition | First Seen (Week) |
|------|------------|--------------------|
| Yield Curve | A graph showing interest rates across different maturities for similar-quality bonds. | – |
